Recipe Nasi Goreng

Home | Contact

Nasi goreng is a dish consisting of rice fried in oil, margarine, or butter, typically seasoned with sweet soy sauce, shallots, garlic, and chili. This iconic Indonesian dish offers a savory, slightly sweet, and often spicy flavor profile, and is usually served with eggs, crackers, and pickles.


The main ingredient

  • White rice (2 plates; use cold or firm-textured rice to prevent it from becoming mushy).

Finely Ground / Sliced ​Spices

  • Shallots (4 cloves).
  • Garlic (2 cloves).
  • Red chilies/bird's eye chilies (adjust to taste).

Sauce & Flavoring Ingredients

  • Sweet soy sauce (1–2 tablespoons).
  • Oyster sauce (1 teaspoon, optional for extra savoriness).
  • Salt (to taste).
  • Flavor enhancer/bouillon powder (to taste).
  • Cooking oil/margarine (2 tablespoons for sautéing).

Toppings

  • Chicken eggs (1–2, scrambled or fried).
  • Shredded chicken meat (as needed).
  • Meatballs or sausages (thinly sliced).
  • Scallion (1 stalk, coarsely sliced).

Serving Suggestions

  • Crackers (as needed).
  • Cucumber and tomato (sliced, served raw).
  • Fried onions (for sprinkling).

Preparation

  1. Mash the shallots, garlic, and chilies into a smooth paste.
  2. Slice the meatballs, sausages, and green onions.
  3. Whisk the eggs in a small bowl with a pinch of salt.

Cooking Steps

  1. Heat one tablespoon of oil in a wok or frying pan.
  2. Scramble the eggs until cooked, then remove them and set aside.
  3. Add the remaining oil or margarine to the same wok.
  4. Sauté the mashed spice paste until fragrant and thoroughly cooked.
  5. Toss in the sliced chicken, meatballs, or sausages.
  6. Stir until the meat is fully cooked.
  7. Turn the heat up to high.
  8. Pour in the cold rice and the scrambled eggs.
  9. Mix everything quickly to break up any rice clumps.
  10. Season with sweet soy sauce, oyster sauce, salt, and broth powder.
  11. Stir-fry vigorously until the rice is evenly coated and slightly smoky.
  12. Throw in the sliced green onions just before turning off the heat.
  13. Mix for another 10 seconds.

Serving

  1. Plate the fried rice while it is hot.
  2. Top with a fried egg and a sprinkle of crispy fried shallots.
  3. Serve with crackers, cucumber slices, and tomato slices on the side.

Recipe information

Prep Time:
10 minutes (for peeling, grinding spices, and slicing toppings).
Cook time
10 minutes (from sautéing the paste to frying the rice).
Servings:
1–2 servings.
Difficulty Level:
Easy. Perfect for beginners since it requires basic cooking skills and common ingredients.
